Basics
Getting Started with LargeNote
📄

Create a Document

Click New in the toolbar to start a blank document. Use the ribbon to format text, insert tables, images, and more.

💾

Save Your Work

Hit Save to save in native .largenote format, or Save As to export as .docx for Word / Google Docs compatibility.

⬜⬜

Split View

Click Split in the top bar to write your document and work on a spreadsheet side by side. Drag the divider to resize each pane.

▶

Slideshow / Sunum

Click Sunum in the ribbon to turn your document into a presentation. Headings become slides automatically.

🌙

Night Mode

Toggle the moon icon in the toolbar to switch between light and dark mode for comfortable writing in any lighting.

🔍

Focus Mode

Click Focus to hide all distractions and write in a clean, minimal environment. Press Escape or click Exit Focus to return.

FAQ
Frequently Asked Questions
What file formats does LargeNote support?
LargeNote saves documents in its native .largenote format and can export to .docx (Microsoft Word / Google Docs compatible). You can open existing files via File → Open.
How do I use Split View?
Click the Split button at the top of the app. The left pane shows your document and the right pane shows a spreadsheet. Drag the vertical divider bar between them to adjust the width of each pane.
How do I create a slideshow from my document?
Use Heading 1 or Heading 2 styles in your document — each heading becomes a slide. Then click ▶ Sunum in the ribbon toolbar. Navigate between slides using the Önceki / Sonraki buttons, or enable auto-advance mode.
Can I use spreadsheet formulas?
Yes. Click any cell and type a formula starting with =, for example =SUM(A1:A5), =AVERAGE(B1:B10), =IF(A1>0,"Yes","No"), or =VLOOKUP(...). LargeNote uses the HyperFormula engine for full formula support.
How do I change the page size or margins?
Use the A4 dropdown in the toolbar to switch between A4, Letter, and Wide page sizes. Margin options are available in the page settings section of the toolbar.
What is Hand Mode?
Hand Mode lets you click and drag to pan the document canvas freely — like a hand tool in design apps. Toggle it on/off with the Hand button in the toolbar.
Is my data stored in the cloud?
No. LargeNote is fully offline. All your documents are saved locally on your Mac. We do not collect, upload, or transmit your data anywhere.
LargeNote won't open — what do I do?
If macOS blocks LargeNote from opening, right-click the app icon and choose Open, then click Open in the dialog. This is a one-time step required for apps not downloaded from the Mac App Store.
